Hindi Bargaining Phrases — Common Expressions Used in Indian Bazaars

Opening and Greeting Phrases:
"Namaste bhaiya" (नमस्ते भैया) — "Hello brother" — A warm, respectful greeting for a male vendor. Using "bhaiya" builds rapport immediately.
"Namaste didi" (नमस्ते दीदी) — "Hello sister" — A respectful greeting for a female vendor. Shows cultural awareness.
"Kitne ka hai?" (कितने का है?) — "How much is this?" — The universal opener for any bargaining conversation. Point at the item while asking.
"Ye dikhao" (ये दिखाओ) — "Show me this" — Used to ask the vendor to show a specific item. The vendor will bring it closer for inspection.
"Kya hai ye?" (क्या है ये?) — "What is this?" — Used when you encounter an unfamiliar item. Shows genuine curiosity.

Price Reaction Phrases:
"Bahut mehnga hai!" (बहुत महंगा है!) — "Too expensive!" — Show shock and disbelief at the first price. This is expected and is the standard response to the opening price. Exaggerate your reaction for effect.
"Itna mehnga!" (इतना महंगा!) — "This expensive!" — A shorter way to express shock. Often accompanied by putting the item down.
"Paagal hai kya?" (पागल है क्या?) — "Are you crazy?" — A more dramatic (but commonly used) reaction to an absurdly high price. Use with a smile to keep it friendly.
"Aur kuch nahi?" (और कुछ नहीं?) — "Nothing else?" — When you want to see more options, possibly cheaper ones.

Negotiation Phrases:
"Thoda kam karo" (थोड़ा कम करो) — "Reduce it a bit" — The polite, gentle way to start negotiating. It signals that you are interested but the price is not right.
"Kuch discount do na" (कुछ डिस्काउंट दो ना) — "Give some discount, please" — A friendly request for a reduction. The "na" at the end softens the request.
"Do sau mein de do" (दो सौ में दे दो) — "Give it for 200" — Replace the number with your counter-offer. Direct and specific counter-offering.
"Sab jagah yahi milta hai" (सब जगह यही मिलता है) — "This is available everywhere" — Undermines the vendor's claim of uniqueness. Implies you can get the same item elsewhere for less.
"Pados wali dukaan mein sasta mil raha hai" (पड़ोस वाली दुकान में सस्ता मिल रहा है) — "The neighboring shop has it cheaper" — Competitive pressure. The vendor may match or beat the other price.

Closing and Walk-Away Phrases:
"Last price bolo" (लास्ट प्राइस बोलो) — "Tell me the final price" — Signals seriousness. You are ready to buy if the price is right. The vendor knows you mean business.
"Chhod do yaar" (छोड़ दो यार) — "Forget it, man" — The walk-away bluff. Start turning away. The vendor will often call you back with a better offer.
"Chalo, chhodo" (चलो, छोड़ो) — "Let's go, leave it" — Said to a companion, indicating you are leaving. Strong walk-away signal.
"Bhaiya, student hoon" (भैया, स्टूडेंट हूँ) — "Brother, I am a student" — The sympathy card. Implies a tight budget. Many vendors soften prices for students.
"Budget nahi hai" (बजट नहीं है) — "I don't have the budget" — A straightforward way to say you cannot afford the price. Vendors may reduce significantly.
"Theek hai, le lunga" (ठीक है, ले लूँगा) — "Okay, I will take it" — Acceptance of the final agreed price. The deal is done.

Emotional and Rapport-Building Phrases:
"Bhaiya meherbaani karo" (भैया मेहरबानी करो) — "Brother, please be kind" — Emotional appeal for a lower price. Works well with friendly vendors.
"Pehli kharidaari hai" (पहली खरीदारी है) — "This is my first purchase" — Many vendors consider the first sale of the day auspicious and may offer a better price.
"Aap ki dukaan bohot acchi hai" (आप की दुकान बोहोत अच्छी है) — "Your shop is very nice" — Flattery helps. Complimenting the shop or merchandise builds goodwill.
"Ek aur le lun toh kya doge?" (एक और ले लूँ तो क्या दोगे?) — "If I take one more, what will you offer?" — Bundling strategy. Buying multiple items to negotiate a better overall price.
"Cash mein de raha hoon" (कैश में दे रहा हूँ) — "I am paying in cash" — Cash payments may earn a small discount since the vendor avoids digital transaction fees.
