[Hindi Phrases: Opening and Greeting at Sabzi Mandi]
"Namaste bhaiya" -- Hello brother. Warm, respectful greeting for male vendor. Using "bhaiya" builds instant rapport.
"Namaste didi" -- Hello sister. Respectful greeting for female vendor. Shows cultural awareness.
"Kaise ho bhaiya?" -- How are you, brother? Personal touch before business. Vendors appreciate this warmth.
"Aaj kya kya taaza aaya hai?" -- What all is fresh today? Shows you're a smart buyer who cares about quality.
"Kitne ka hai?" -- How much is this? Universal opener for any bargaining conversation. Point at the item while asking.
"Ye dikhao" -- Show me this. Used to ask vendor to bring item closer for inspection.
"Aur kya kya hai?" -- What else do you have? Signals interest in buying multiple items.
"Bhaiya, sab taaza hai na?" -- Brother, everything is fresh right? Puts vendor on notice that you'll check quality.

[Hindi Phrases: Quality Check and Inspection]
"Ye taaza hai?" -- Is this fresh? Question freshness directly. Vendor must prove it.
"Ye kitne din purana hai?" -- How many days old is this? Forces vendor to admit stock age.
"Chhukar dekh sakta hoon?" -- Can I touch and check? Asking permission to inspect. Respectful but expected in sabzi mandi.
"Thoda purana lag raha hai" -- It looks a bit old. Polite way to question freshness for a lower price.
"Bhaiya, ye wali nahi, peeche se fresh dena" -- Not these, give fresh ones from the back. Smart buyers know vendors hide fresh stock.
"Ye toh gala hua hai!" -- This is rotten! Strong complaint. Use when quality is genuinely bad.
"Kal ka lag raha hai ye" -- This looks like yesterday's stock. Mid-level freshness complaint.
"Bhaiya achhi wali chun ke dena" -- Brother, pick the good ones for me. Polite request for quality selection.
"Tol ke dikhao" -- Show me the weighing. Ensures vendor isn't shortchanging on weight.

[Hindi Phrases: Price Reaction and Shock]
"Bahut mehnga hai!" -- Too expensive! Standard first reaction to ANY quoted price. Exaggerate shock for effect. This is expected.
"Itna mehnga!" -- This expensive! Shorter shock phrase. Often accompanied by putting the produce down dramatically.
"Paagal hai kya?" -- Are you crazy? Dramatic but commonly used reaction to absurd prices. Use with a SMILE to keep it friendly.
"Ye toh loot hai!" -- This is robbery! Stronger expression of disbelief. Implies the price is outrageously unfair.
"Bhaiya, itne mein toh imported apple aa jaaye!" -- At that price I'd get imported apples! Comparing the item to something much more expensive to highlight absurdity.
"Sach bolo, kitne ka doge?" -- Tell me seriously, how much will you give it for? Signals you're cutting through the performance.
"Saamne wala ₹40 mein de raha hai" -- The stall across is selling at ₹40. Competitive pricing reference.
"Mandi rate pata hai mujhe" -- I know the wholesale rate. Signals buyer knowledge. Vendor lowers guard and price.

[Hindi Phrases: Negotiation and Counter-Offer]
"Thoda kam karo" -- Reduce it a bit. Polite, gentle way to start negotiating. Signals interest.
"Kuch discount do na" -- Give some discount, please. Friendly request. The "na" at the end softens the ask.
"Chaalees mein de do" -- Give it for ₹40. Direct counter-offer. Replace the number with your bid.
"Sab jagah yahi rate hai" -- It's the same rate everywhere. Undermines vendor's uniqueness claim on pricing.
"Pados wali dukaan mein sasta mil raha hai" -- The neighboring shop has it cheaper. Competitive pressure tactic.
"Bhaiya, roz ayenge" -- Brother, I'll come daily. Signals long-term business. Vendors value repeat customers over one-time profit.
"2 kilo le raha hoon, thoda kam karo" -- I'm buying 2 kg, reduce a bit. Bulk buying leverage.
"Cash mein de raha hoon" -- I'm paying in cash. Cash payments earn 2-5% discount. Vendors avoid digital fees.
"Sab mila ke kitna hoga?" -- How much total for everything? Bundle deal request for multiple items.
"Ek kilo tamatar aur 1 kilo pyaaz dono ₹60 mein de do" -- Give both 1kg tomatoes and 1kg onions for ₹60. Combo pricing strategy.

[Hindi Phrases: Free Extras and Bundles]
"Bhaiya dhaniya daal do" -- Brother, throw in coriander. Standard request for free coriander with vegetable purchase. Almost always granted for orders over ₹100.
"Thodi mirch bhi daal do" -- Add some green chilies too. Another common free extra request.
"Nimbu bhi de do ek-do" -- Give a couple of lemons too. Free lemon ask with bigger purchases.
"Bhaiya sab le raha hoon, kuch extra daal do" -- I'm buying everything, throw in something extra. General bonus request for large orders.
"Ye bhi le lunga agar thoda aur kam karo" -- I'll take this too if you reduce a bit more. Conditional bundle expansion.

[Hindi Phrases: Walk-Away and Final Offers]
"Last price bolo" -- Tell me the final price. Signals seriousness. The vendor knows you mean business now.
"Chhod do yaar" -- Forget it, man. The walk-away bluff. Start turning away. Vendor often calls you back.
"Chalo, chhodo" -- Let's go, leave it. Said to companion while leaving. Strong walk-away signal.
"Theek hai, nahi chahiye" -- Fine, I don't want it. Firm walk-away. Use only if you mean it.
"Dusri dukaan se le lunga" -- I'll buy from another stall. Ultimate competitive threat.
"Budget nahi hai" -- I don't have the budget. Straightforward budget constraint. Vendors may reduce significantly.
"Bhaiya meherbaani karo" -- Brother, please be kind. Emotional appeal for compassion discount.
"Ye lo final, isse zyada nahi dunga" -- Take this, I won't pay more. Your final stand. Mean it.
"Kal phir aa jaunga" -- I'll come again tomorrow. Gentle walk-away. Vendor may stop you with a better deal now.

[Hindi Phrases: Closing the Deal]
"Theek hai, le lunga" -- Okay, I'll take it. Acceptance of final price. Deal is done.
"Theli mein daal do" -- Put it in a bag. Confirming the purchase. Transaction closing phrase.
"Shukriya bhaiya, phir ayenge" -- Thanks brother, I'll come again. Builds goodwill for future visits.
"Bahut taaza hai aaj" -- Very fresh today. Post-purchase compliment about produce quality. Makes vendor happy.
"Aapki dukaan bohot acchi hai" -- Your shop is very nice. Flattery that builds lasting rapport.
"Pehli kharidaari hai" -- This is my first purchase. Many vendors consider the first sale of the day auspicious and offer better prices ("bohni").
"Done! Pakka!" -- Done! Confirmed! Quick, decisive deal-closing. Both sides walk away satisfied.

[Hindi Phrases: Vendor's Common Lines at Sabzi Mandi (What You'll Hear)]
"Arre sahab, aao aao!" -- Come, come sir! Standard welcoming call from vendors.
"Aaj ka maal hai, subah aaya hai" -- Today's stock, came in the morning. Freshness claim. May or may not be true.
"Ye toh cost price hai" -- This is the cost price. NEVER true. Standard vendor claim. Real cost is 30-50% lower.
"Aapke liye special rate" -- Special rate just for you. Every customer hears this. Not actually special.
"Itne mein toh mera khana bhi nahi niklega" -- I can't even eat at that price. Standard dramatic response to low offers. It's theater.
"Aur koi nahi dega itne mein" -- Nobody else will give you this price. Vendor trying to prevent comparison.
"Pehla grahak ho, bohni hai" -- You're the first customer, it's auspicious. Used to justify giving a "special" first-sale price.
"Dekho kitna taaza hai, haath mein lo" -- See how fresh it is, hold it. Tactic to make you feel attached to the produce.
"Aaj rate badh gaye hain" -- Prices went up today. May be genuine (mandi prices fluctuate daily) or a negotiation tactic.
"Le lo le lo, sham ko toh aur mehnga hoga" -- Buy now, it'll be more expensive by evening. Urgency creation tactic. Usually FALSE -- evening prices DROP for perishables.
